# Program Audit Team: Roles & Responsibilities

## Overview

This team will be responsible for verifying the accuracy of all program
information against official government sources, ensuring data consistency, and
identifying any gaps or errors in the system. This document outlines the structure, roles, and
responsibilities required to conduct a comprehensive audit of all immigration
program data and flowcharts within the system. The audit covers **27 EU country
programs** with multiple visa categories per country, including work visas,
skilled worker permits, EU Blue Cards, freelance visas, family reunification,
digital nomad visas, golden visas, and more.

---

## Team Structure

### 1. Audit Lead / Project Manager

**Headcount:** 1

**Responsibilities:**
- Define audit scope, timeline, and deliverables
- Coordinate between all team members and stakeholders
- Manage audit progress tracking and reporting
- Escalate critical issues to business leadership
- Ensure audit completion within budget and timeline
- Sign off on final audit report

**Qualifications:**
- Project management certification (PMP or equivalent)
- Experience managing compliance or data quality projects
- Strong organizational and communication skills

---

### 2. Immigration Policy Specialists

**Headcount:** 3–4 (regional coverage)

**Responsibilities:**
- Verify accuracy of all visa program requirements against official government sources
- Validate eligibility criteria, document requirements, and processing timelines
- Confirm salary thresholds, financial requirements, and fee structures
- Cross-reference flowchart steps with current immigration law
- Identify outdated information or policy changes
- Document discrepancies with source citations

**Regional Coverage:**
- Western Europe (DE, FR, NL, BE, LU, AT)
- Southern Europe (ES, PT, IT, GR, MT, CY)
- Northern & Eastern Europe (IE, DK, SE, FI, EE, LV, LT, PL, CZ, SK, HU, SI, HR, BG, RO)

**Qualifications:**
- Immigration law background or paralegal certification
- Fluency in relevant regional languages preferred
- Experience with EU immigration policies

---

### 3. Data Quality Analyst

**Headcount:** 1–2

**Responsibilities:**
- Validate data consistency across all program files
- Ensure template usage is correct and standardized
- Verify country configurations match program-specific data
- Check for missing, duplicate, or conflicting information
- Validate step IDs, template references, and document lists
- Generate data quality metrics and exception reports

**Qualifications:**
- Experience with data validation and quality assurance
- Familiarity with TypeScript/JavaScript data structures
- Strong attention to detail

---

### 4. Technical QA Engineer

**Headcount:** 1

**Responsibilities:**
- Verify flowchart syntax and structure (Mermaid diagrams)
- Ensure all flowchart nodes are properly connected
- Validate step references match defined step IDs
- Test flowchart rendering and navigation
- Confirm builder functions produce valid output
- Run existing test suites and report failures

**Qualifications:**
- Software QA experience
- Familiarity with Mermaid diagram syntax
- TypeScript/JavaScript proficiency

---

### 5. UX/Content Reviewer

**Headcount:** 1

**Responsibilities:**
- Review step titles, descriptions, and notes for clarity
- Ensure consistent tone and terminology across programs
- Verify instructions are actionable and user-friendly
- Identify jargon or confusing language
- Recommend improvements for user comprehension

**Qualifications:**
- Technical writing or UX writing experience
- Understanding of immigration processes helpful
- Strong editorial skills

---

### 6. Legal Compliance Officer

**Headcount:** 1 (part-time or consultant)

**Responsibilities:**
- Ensure program information does not constitute legal advice
- Verify appropriate disclaimers are in place
- Review for regulatory compliance risks
- Advise on liability considerations
- Sign off on legal review of audit findings

**Qualifications:**
- Licensed attorney or compliance professional
- Immigration law knowledge preferred

---

## Audit Deliverables

1. **Country Audit Reports** — Per-country findings with issue severity ratings
2. **Consolidated Exception Log** — All discrepancies, errors, and outdated information
3. **Data Quality Scorecard** — Metrics on accuracy, completeness, and consistency
4. **Recommended Corrections** — Prioritized list of required updates
5. **Final Audit Summary** — Executive summary with sign-off from Audit Lead

---

## Phases of The Audit

| Phase | Activities |
|-------|------------|
| Planning | Define scope, assign countries, gather source materials |
| Data Collection | Pull official government sources for all programs |
| Verification | Cross-reference program data against sources |
| Technical Review | Validate flowcharts, templates, and code structure |
| Content Review | UX and clarity review of all user-facing text |
| Legal Review | Compliance and liability review |
| Reporting | Compile findings, generate reports, present to stakeholders |

---

## Success Criteria

- 100% of programs reviewed against official sources
- All critical discrepancies resolved or documented with remediation plan
- Data quality score of 95%+ achieved
- Legal sign-off obtained
- Audit report approved by business leadership
